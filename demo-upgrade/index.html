01 — First Impression

The hero does more heavy lifting

The current hero relies on the logo + floating emojis to carry the page. The upgraded hero adds a real product shot (that cookie platter speaks louder than any headline), tightens the copy so the first thing a visitor reads tells Google and the customer exactly what the business does — handcrafted cookies, made from scratch in Tyndall, pre-order pickup every Wednesday.

Upgraded Site

SEO-first subtitle

"Handcrafted cookies made from scratch in Tyndall, MB — pre-order pickup every Wednesday" hits every search term someone would Google.

Clearer next step

"See Our Flavours" beats "View Gallery" — matches the way a customer actually thinks about what to order.

02 — NEW  Reassurance Bar

A trust strip that answers the silent questions

Right under the hero, a thin olive strip with four micro-badges: Tyndall MB · Pickup every Wednesday · Made fresh from scratch · Pre-order only, min 3pc. Four questions a first-time customer silently has — answered in under two seconds of scrolling.

New section on the upgraded site

Kills the location question

"Where are you based?" is the #1 DM. The strip answers it before they can ask — and filters out customers who aren't near Tyndall.

Sets the pickup expectation

"Wednesday pickups" sits up front so nobody fills out the form expecting next-day cookies. Fewer "can I get these tomorrow?" messages.

Signals a real operation

A structured micro-bar visually tells visitors this is a business with rules and standards — not a casual side hustle.

03 — Flavour of the Month

Urgency on the monthly drop

The Flavour of the Month is already one of the strongest conversion levers on the site. The upgrade adds a "Limited — April Only" urgency pill with a pulsing dot, so visitors know this flavour disappears at month-end and orders rush in now instead of "later."

Current
Upgraded

Scarcity drives action

"Limited — April Only" gives the visitor a reason to order today. Every day they wait, the flavour gets closer to gone.

Pulsing dot grabs the eye

A subtle animated indicator — not shouty, just enough to pull focus to the urgency line without cheapening the brand.

Easy monthly swap

When May rolls around, the copy swaps to "Limited — May Only" with the new flavour. Same pattern, zero rebuild.

04 — Social Proof

Real customer quotes above the videos

The current testimonials section shows real customer videos + screenshots — strong visual proof. The upgrade adds three short text quote cards above the carousel, so readers get a hook in text form before they scroll through video reviews. Scan-friendly for fast readers, proof-heavy for slow ones.

Current
Upgraded

Text beats video for scanners

Most visitors don't watch review videos — they skim. Pull quotes catch the scanners. Video still rewards the deep divers.

Three angles, one section

One quote covers flavour. One covers gifting (birthday). One covers quality ("best in Manitoba"). Covers the three reasons people buy.

Placeholders swap to real ones

Current quotes are placeholders. Once you send 3 real customer quotes over, we drop them straight in — same design, real names.

05 — Order Form

Same form, added trust signal

The order form is already doing the heavy lifting — per-flavour quantity selectors, live pricing summary, automatic pickup-date logic. The upgrade adds one small but critical line right under the submit button: "No payment taken until Shai confirms your order." Kills the biggest hesitation first-time buyers have on a home-based bakery site.

Current
Upgraded

Kills the "am I about to get charged?" fear

New visitors hesitate before hitting submit because they don't know if the form will auto-charge them. The shield-check line removes that.

"Reserve My Cookies" reads warmer

First-person copy on the submit button frames it as their decision, not a transaction. Friendly, not clinical.

Less back-and-forth over e-transfer

Customers now expect Shai to confirm first — so nobody sends money before the order is accepted. Fewer awkward refunds.

06 — Polish Pass

The tiny upgrades that add up

Individually small, collectively the difference between "looks nice" and "looks professional." Every emoji swapped for a crisp Lucide icon, consistent Canadian spelling, clearer button copy. None of these alone would move the needle — together, they lift the feel of the whole site.

Icons

Emojis → Lucide icons

Across About, Contact, and the order form, platform-dependent emojis are swapped for crisp Lucide SVG icons that render identically on every device and browser.

Copy

"Flavors" → "Flavours"

Consistent Canadian spelling across the whole site. Small detail, but a Winnipeg customer notices when it's wrong.

Gallery

BTS marquee row

A second scrolling row below the cookie photos showing behind-the-scenes content — dough prep, packaging, the process. Builds trust and character.

CTA

Instagram icon on gallery CTA

The "See More on Instagram" button gets a Lucide Instagram icon — signals clearly where the click leads before they tap it.

07 — Under The Hood

The invisible upgrades — the ones Google cares about

Changes you won't see at a glance, but the ones that separate a hobby site from a professional one. These affect search ranking, load speed on mobile, and who can actually find the site in the first place.

SEO

SEO-optimized title + meta description

"Sugar & Shai Cookies | Homemade Cookies · Winnipeg, MB" — tells Google exactly what the site is and where it serves. Pairs with a keyword-rich meta description for the search snippet.

SEO

Bakery schema for Google

JSON-LD LocalBusiness markup of type Bakery — signals to Google that this is a Winnipeg-area bakery, unlocking "cookies near me" and map-pack eligibility.

SEO

Open Graph + Twitter Card

When the link gets shared over text, IG DMs, or Facebook, it now shows a proper preview card with a cookie image and headline — not a broken thumbnail.

Speed

Lazy-loaded images

Gallery, about slideshow, and reviews all load only when they scroll into view. First paint happens faster — especially on slow mobile data.

Speed

Hero image preload

The product platter in the hero is preloaded so it paints with the rest of the hero — no awkward image pop-in on first scroll.

Speed

Deferred GSAP loading

Animation library loads after the page content paints — content shows first, animations sprinkle in after. Feels faster even when it's not.

Accessibility

Descriptive image alt text

Every image has a real description — "Biscoff and matcha cookies," "Mini eggs cookies" — so screen readers (and Google image search) can parse them.

Trust

Confirm-before-pay flow spelled out

Shield-check line on the submit button sets the expectation: nobody sends an e-transfer before Shai confirms. Fewer chargebacks, fewer awkward refunds.
